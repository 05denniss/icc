Cultural Comparison

Germany & Russia

A comparison of how differently everyday life, values, and social norms are lived in Germany and Russia according to stereotypes. Part of the ICC submission by Dennis Nikitin and Felix Teufel.

6 cultural aspects in comparison

Work and Punctuality
Germany
Time is a fixed, plannable resource. Being unpunctual is considered disrespectful – appointments are kept to the minute.
Russia
Time is flexible and subordinated to interpersonal priorities. "5 minutes late" can mean half an hour – and that is accepted.
Studying and Exams
Germany
Exam performances are individual achievements. Personal responsibility and individual preparation are paramount. Cheating is considered a serious rule violation.
Russia
Mutual support in the group is a matter of honor – even during exams. The collective often takes precedence over rules.
Party and Hospitality
Germany
Parties are often "Bring your own" – everyone brings their own alcohol. The host provides the space, and the costs are shared.
Russia
The host fills the table to the brim – it's a matter of honor. Guests should help themselves and never feel like there is too little.
Encounters in Public
Germany
Germans smile at strangers in a friendly but non-committal way. Superficial politeness is standard – deeper connections form slowly.
Russia
Russians only smile when they have a genuine reason. In return, friendships are intense, physical, and loyal.
Hierarchy in the Office
Germany
Decisions are often made through discussion and consensus. Employees actively contribute ideas – flat hierarchies are the norm in many companies.
Russia
Authority and hierarchy are highly respected. Top-down decisions are the rule – the boss decides, the others execute.
Criticism & Feedback
Germany
Germans clearly separate the factual and relationship levels. Direct, factual criticism is considered constructive and professional.
Russia
Factual criticism is often perceived as a personal attack. Feedback must be expressed carefully, as it might be received negatively.
